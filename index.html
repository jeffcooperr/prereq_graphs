01 / 03
Inspiration

As a freshman I came across this CS prerequisite chart and found it useful. Seeing the full dependency tree helped me think ahead about what paths I could take as an undergrad. What surprised me was that this wasn't a standard feature for students in every department.

02 / 03
Method

No structured prerequisite data was available, so I pulled from the Schedule of Courses, where each class description usually includes a brief note about prerequisites. That text is inconsistently formatted and not a trivial parsing problem, so I ran each course description through Gemini 2.5 Flash to extract the relationships.

Because this relies on an LLM interpreting free-form text, there's no guarantee every relationship was captured correctly. Treat the graphs as a helpful reference, not a source of truth. There can also be discrepancies within the same course description...

Also note that classes are often listed as prereqs in the SOC, but may not actually be offered during either of the Fall/Spring 2026 semesters, which were used to build the graphs.

Work in progress

Until this is more polished and less overwhelming, think of it as an explorable visual; something to poke around in and get a feel for how courses connect across a department.

That said, browsing the prerequisite chains for your major could give you a useful head start when it comes time to pick classes. That's the goal, at least.
